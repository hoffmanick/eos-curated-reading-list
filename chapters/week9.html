10  FAIR, CARE, and Open Science


10.1 Goal

The EOS RCN contends that alignment with FAIR and CARE is supportive of the movement for Open Science. This module explores the relationship between those data principles and the Open Science movement through two papers which consider how FAIR and CARE relate to each other and two papers which examine the meaning of Open Science for ecology and archaeology.

10.2 Readings

  • Carroll et al., 2021: “Operationalizing the CARE and FAIR Principles for Indigenous data futures”
  • Sterner & Elliott, 2022: “The FAIR and CARE Data Principles Influence Who Counts As a Participant in Biodiversity Science by Governing teh Fitness-for-Use of Data”
  • Hampton et al., 2015: “The Tao of open science for ecology”
  • Marwick et al., 2017: “Open Science in Archaeology”

10.3 Questions

  • How do the FAIR and CARE principles relate to each other? Where are they complementary to each other? Are there any points of tension between them?
  • How do Hampton et al. (2015) and Marwick et al. (2017) describe Open Science? What is valuable about it? How are archaeologists and ecologists supposed to move toward Open Science within their disciplines?
  • Neither Hampton et al. (2015) nor Marwick et al. (2017) explicitly mention the FAIR or CARE principles, yet we posit that there is a relationship between those principles and Open Science which should be recognized. How might FAIR and CARE be supportive of the mission of Open Science?
