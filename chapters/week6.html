7  Indigenous Data Sovereignty and the CARE Principles


7.1 Goal

The CARE principles of Indigenous data governance were developed by a group of data scholars in order to remedy the harms against Indigenous peoples that scientific data sometimes enact. This module introduces the CARE principles as well as some examples of data governance by native nations.

7.2 Readings

  • Carroll et al., 2019: “Indigenous Data Governance: Strategies from United States Native Nations”
  • Carroll et al., 2020: “The CARE Principles of Indigenous Data Governance”

7.3 Questions

  • How do the authors of the CARE Principles recommend that scientists align with CARE? What are the low-hanging fruit of CARE, and what aspects of alignment seem more challenging? What sorts of objects might be considered as CARE-aligned (e.g., scientists, their data, data repositories, universities, etc.) ?
  • What values do the principles enact? What seems to be most important for the authors?
  • What kinds of science, and what kinds of data, do the authors of the CARE principles treat as paradigmatic? What becomes marginal in their vision of the world?
