5  Data and Power


5.1 Goal

The FAIR data principles offer no explicit theory of the relationship between knowledge and power. This module complicates the project of FAIR data by noticing the political economic conditions which have authorized that project and investigating how the technical articulation of data affect the meanings they embed.

5.2 Readings

  • The Future(s) of Open Science
  • Bowker, 2000: “Biodiversity Datadiversity”
  • Devictor & Bensaude-Vincent, 2016: “From ecological records to big data: the invention of global biodiversity”

5.3 Questions

  • What modes of seeing the world and what values are at stake in the creation of biodiversity databases?
  • According to these readings, how does scientific theory affect the meaning of data?
  • Both of these papers suggest that historicizing data can enhance scientific practice as well as understanding of how science works. How might such histories prove helpful?
