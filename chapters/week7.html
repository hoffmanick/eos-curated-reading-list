8  CAREful Quaternary Data Governance


8.1 Goal

Much like the FAIR principles, the CARE principles have fostered vigorous discussion across many disciplines. This module surveys the uptake of CARE by Quaternary scientists.

8.2 Readings

  • Gupta et al., 2023: “The CARE Principles and the Reuse, Sharing, and Curation of Indigenous Data in Canadian Archaeology”
  • Jennings et al., 2023: “Applying the ‘CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance’ to ecology and biodiversity research”
  • McCartney et al., 2023: “Indigenous peoples and local communities as partners in the sequencing of global eukaryotic biodiversity”

8.3 Questions

  • How have archaeologists and ecologists attempted to align with the CARE Principles? Where have they made the most progress? What are the biggest challenges?
  • Where in the data lifecycle is CARE being implemented? What kinds of objects or collectives are made to align with CARE?
  • What tools are common to archaeological and ecological uptake of the principles? How do these two disciplinary communities differ in their implementation choices?
