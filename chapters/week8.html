9  FAIR, CARE, and Data Repositories


9.1 Goal

Concurrent with the rise of big data over the past few decades has been the rise of specialist repositories curating particular kinds of big research data. This module posits these repositories as favorable sites for socializing the FAIR and CARE principles and offers examples of how they have been attempting to move toward alignment with those principles.

9.2 Readings

  • Lehnert & Hsu, 2015: “The New Paradigm of Data Publication”
  • O’Brien et al., 2024: “Earth Science Data Repositories: Implementing the CARE Principles”
  • Jennings et al., 2025: “Governance of Indigenous data in open earth systems science”

9.3 Questions

  • What is the value of specialist repositories? What sorts of factors might influence one’s decision to contribute to a specialist repository as opposed to a generalist repository like Zenodo?
  • How have the repositories described in these readings attempted to implement CARE? Where have they made the most progress and what are the biggest challenges?
  • What special affordancess does a repository have in diffusing data ethical norms, both within its own organizational structure and for the other entities (i.e., data uploaders and funders) with whom the repository interacts?
